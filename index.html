The Mission

Our Mission

Lumina Hub Africa exists to transform unemployed and underemployed youth into employable professionals and job creators by bridging the gap between education and opportunity. We focus on converting learning, potential, and talent into real-world competence through hands-on experience, mentorship, and exposure to practical work environments.

Skills & Confidence

Equip young people with the skills and confidence required to participate meaningfully in the world of work.

Professional Readiness

Build professional readiness through employment, entrepreneurship, or income-generating opportunities.

Real-World Competence

Convert learning and potential into real-world competence through hands-on experience and mentorship.

The Challenge

Education Alone Is Not Enough in Kibera, Kenya

In Kibera and across Kenya, youth unemployment continues to be a major socioeconomic challenge, particularly among young people who have completed secondary, college, or university education but cannot find meaningful work.

43%

of young Kenyans (ages 18–35)

According to the most recent Afrobarometer survey, 43% say they are not employed and are currently looking for work, despite high levels of education.

National labour figures show that overall youth unemployment and underemployment remain significant, with many young people lacking access to consistent income opportunities.

This disconnect stems from:

Theoretical education with limited practical application
Few opportunities to build employer-trusted proof of ability
Limited access to work experience and professional networks

As a result, capable youth remain locked out of the job market — a gap Lumina Hub Africa exists to close.

Who We Serve

Our Target Participants

Primary

University & College Graduates

Unemployed and underemployed university and college graduates aged 21–30

  • Completed or final-year students
  • Limited or no formal work experience
  • Living in Kibera or surrounding communities
  • Motivated to transition into employment
Secondary

High-Potential Youth

High-potential youth without university degrees aged 18–35

  • TVET graduates
  • Self-taught digital learners
  • Dropouts with demonstrated skills
  • Selected through practical challenges

Selection Philosophy: Motivation, discipline, and learning ability matter more than academic history.

Our Approach

Inclusive Program Approach

Lumina Hub Africa is intentionally designed to be inclusive of young people from diverse academic and professional backgrounds. Whether participants come from engineering, medicine, business, agriculture, the arts, or other fields of study, they are all welcome.

Engineering
Medicine
Business
Agriculture
Arts
Social Sciences

The program does not focus on retraining participants in their academic disciplines. Instead, it provides a shared professional foundation—practical skills, real-world application, and proof-of-work—that enables participants to translate their existing knowledge into employable and income-generating opportunities across sectors.

Diversity of background is viewed as a strength, enriching collaboration, problem-solving, and innovation throughout the program.

The Program

The Lumina Jipange Program

A 12-Week Journey from Readiness to Opportunity

Phase 1 • Weeks 1–4

Foundations & Professional Readiness

  • Professional work habits and discipline
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Communication and collaboration
  • Introduction to real-world tasks
  • Mentorship and personal mission alignment

Outcome: Participants develop workplace readiness and professional confidence.

Phase 2 • Weeks 5–8

Applied Skills & Proof-of-Work

  • Digital marketing and opportunity identification
  • Solving real problems for community businesses
  • Portfolio and proof-of-work ledger development
  • Weekly employer field visits and exposure

Outcome: Participants build tangible, employer-reviewable evidence of competence.

Phase 3 • Weeks 9–12

Pathway Specialization

Employment Track
  • Role-aligned portfolio refinement
  • Interview and workplace preparation
  • Internships and job matching
Entrepreneurship Track
  • Business idea testing and validation
  • Customer discovery and pilots
  • Mentorship from founders

Outcome: Participants transition into employment, paid work, or early-stage ventures.

How Personalisation Works

Same Foundation. Different Outcomes.

Lumina does not teach everyone the same skills. All participants share a common professional foundation—work ethic, problem-solving, collaboration, and employer expectations.

Personalisation happens through projects, tracks, and real-world application, not lectures. Participants work on problems aligned with their background, interests, and strengths, producing different outputs within the same structured environment.

Common Foundation

Work ethic, problem-solving, collaboration, employer expectations

Personalised Tracks

Projects aligned with background, interests, and strengths

Unique Outputs

Different portfolios within the same structured environment
